4 · End‑to‑End Saga Walkthrough

What happens between "Place Order" and "Delivered" — service by service.

1 · PLACED
Order Service persists the order in orders_db and publishes order.placed → order-events.
2 · CONFIRMED
Inventory Service consumes the event, reserves stock (optimistic locking + Redis cache), publishes inventory.reserved. Order flips to CONFIRMED.
3 · PAYMENT
Payment Service charges via mock gateway (100–500ms latency, ~10% random failure), publishes payment.success / payment.failed.
4 · SHIPPED
Shipping Service creates a shipment + tracking number, simulates delivery, publishes shipment.created then delivery events.
5 · DELIVERED
Notification emails the customer at every step; Analytics aggregates metrics and Kafka lag. Order reaches DELIVERED.
✕ COMPENSATION
If payment fails → reserved stock is released and order becomes PAYMENT_FAILED. If stock is unavailable → order is CANCELLED. Kafka retains events so a crashed service reprocesses on restart (idempotency keys prevent duplicates).
